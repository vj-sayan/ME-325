# ME325-Low-Cost Lightweight Tea Leaf Collection Bucket

## Author
Team Ergonomics (Arulram - E/21/037 | E/21/094 | E/21/365)[cite: 2]

## Description
**Need:** To design, build, and test a ventilated and ergonomically structured tea leaf collection bucket to study the mechanisms of heat reduction and load distribution[cite: 2].

**Aim:** To improve harvesting productivity of tea leaves and worker comfort by replacing currently available baskets with an optimized, low-cost solution[cite: 2].

## Main Features
* **Air Flow:** Features engineered targeted ventilation and a ventilated cavity to prevent internal heat buildup and leaf fermentation[cite: 2].
* **Capacity:** Aims to safely handle 10-11 kg while using lightweight materials[cite: 2].
* **Leaf Quality:** Uses rigid support bars to maintain structural integrity, preventing the crushing and heat-induced permutation of bottom leaves[cite: 2].
* **Ergonomics:** Applies specialized support padding and integrated shoulder structural straps to eliminate localized strain and distribute loads[cite: 2].
* **Cost:** Aims to reduce manufacturing cost to Rs. 1000, making it economically viable[cite: 2].

## Engineering Tools & Methodology
* **Software:** SolidWorks for CAD design of the bucket frame and ventilation slots, and ANSYS for thermal flow analysis and structural stress testing[cite: 2].
* **Methodology:** Includes ergonomic baseline studies, CAD modeling of the internal support frame, selection of low-cost synthetic materials, and field visualization testing[cite: 2].

## Impact
* **Social:** Eliminating chronic shoulder pain and fatigue improves the overall wellbeing of plantation communities[cite: 2].
* **Quality:** Drastically reduces crop waste and improves final tea quality by preventing crushing[cite: 2].
* **Economic:** Yields higher daily productivity and provides massive economic relief to plantation owners at a fleet scale[cite: 2].
